MANILA, Philippines – President Duterte prefers a federal-parliamentary form of government, his chief congressional ally Speaker Pantaleon Alvarez said yesterday.

“He has expressed the view that it would be best that we have a federal-parliamentary system with a prime minister but with a president directly elected by the people,” Alvarez said in a television interview.

He said members of parliament would elect from among themselves the prime minister.

“The President will be in charge of national defense, foreign affairs, maybe the national police and monetary policies. The prime minister will be like the chief operating officer of a company. It’s a question of delineating their powers,” he added.

Alvarez pointed out that it would not be good if the nation would have only a prime minister, since “he may be ousted anytime by a vote of no-confidence by the parliament.”

“So if you have a president directly elected by the people, someone still holds power,” he stressed.

He said there are various federal-parliamentary models that lawmakers sitting as a constituent assembly (con-ass) could study.

“We have certain models that we can consider, like Germany, France and a little from the United States. The beauty of federalism is that you can tailor your system to what will be most applicable to your culture,” he said.

There are at least five resolutions introduced in the House of Representatives calling for Charter change (Cha-cha) to shift the nation to the federal system.

However, the authors differ on the Cha-cha mode and on the type of federal setup that would replace the present presidential form of government.
